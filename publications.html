Journal

On the Relationship Between Acoustic Absorption and Temperature Gradient in a Thermoacoustic Liner

Biswas, S., Kresl, W., & Manimala, J. M. — International Journal of Aeroacoustics, Vol. 24, Issue 1-2, pp. 42-67.

📖 3 citations 📅 2025

This study investigates the coupled relationship between acoustic absorption and thermal gradient generation in a thermoacoustic liner. NASA Langley Research Center collaboration achieved a 9.5°C peak thermal gradient at an optimized frequency of 790 Hz, with acoustic absorption coefficients exceeding 0.8 across the tested frequency range. Parametric studies using NIT tube experiments and DeltaEC simulations quantified the liner performance under varying stack geometries and acoustic driving conditions.

Multifunctional Characterization of a Thermoacoustic Meta-Structure

Biswas, S., Krawczyk, Z., & Manimala, J. M. — Progress in Engineering Science, p. 100147.

📅 2025

Comprehensive characterization of TAMS multifunctional performance demonstrating simultaneous noise mitigation (15–25 dB reduction over 100–2000 Hz) and microwatt-scale power generation. The meta-structure integrates thermoacoustic, piezoelectric, and resonant absorption mechanisms into a single unified architecture, validated through experimental testing and COMSOL Multiphysics simulations.

Patent

THERMOACOUSTIC META-STRUCTURE

Manimala, J., & Biswas, S. — International Patent WO 2025/128,348 A1.

📅 Filed 2024, Published 2025

A novel thermoacoustic meta-structure device that simultaneously achieves broadband noise mitigation and acoustic energy harvesting. The patented design incorporates spiral and helical resonator geometries with thermoacoustic stack elements enabling passive conversion of acoustic energy into thermal gradients and subsequently to electrical power. Validated at DFW Airport with 5+ dB noise reduction.

Journal

Quiet Power: Exploring the Feasibility of a Noise-Mitigating, Thermoacoustic Energy Harvester

Biswas, S., & Manimala, J. M. — Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, Vol. 151, Issue 4.

📖 3 citations 📅 2022

Peer-reviewed journal paper establishing the theoretical framework and experimental validation of TAMS. Demonstrates that carefully engineered acoustic meta-structures can simultaneously attenuate broadband noise while harvesting thermal energy from acoustic-to-thermal conversion. Key findings include 15–25 dB noise reduction across 100–2000 Hz and microwatt-level power generation from ambient acoustic excitation, validated in both anechoic chamber and reverberant room environments.

Conf.

Evaluation of Additively Manufactured Stacks for Thermo-Acoustic Devices

Biswas, S., Krawczyk, Z., & Manimala, J. M. — INTER-NOISE, Vol. 263, pp. 4376-4387.

📖 4 citations 📅 2021

Comparative evaluation of additively manufactured polymer stacks versus traditional ceramic stacks in thermoacoustic devices. DeltaEC simulations and experimental measurements assess acoustic performance, thermal gradient generation, and manufacturability. Results demonstrate that FDM-printed stacks can achieve comparable thermoacoustic performance to ceramic stacks while enabling complex geometry optimization impossible with conventional manufacturing — enabling TAMS geometry iteration at significantly reduced cost and time.

Thesis

Structural Power Storage Composite with Planar & Non-planar Form Factor

Biswas, S. — Master's Thesis, Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, Arizona.

📅 2019 🎓 Northern Arizona University

Master's thesis investigating the design and fabrication of structural supercapacitors for electric vehicle applications. Carbon fiber electrodes functionalized via CVD and plasma treatment achieve 53.58 mWh/kg energy density and 32.06 W/kg power density while maintaining 6.9 GPa flexural modulus — demonstrating that load-bearing structural components can simultaneously serve as energy storage elements, reducing overall vehicle mass and improving efficiency.
