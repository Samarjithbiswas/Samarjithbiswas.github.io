Orbital Compute — Space-Based Data Centers

By 2028, data centers will consume 12% of U.S. electricity. Orbital Compute leverages sun-synchronous orbit for >95% solar illumination and 2.7K cosmic heat sink—delivering a 20x cost advantage over terrestrial facilities at an estimated $0.002/kWh.

Solar Illumination

>95% solar availability in sun-synchronous orbit eliminates the intermittency problem of terrestrial renewables for continuous compute operations.

Cosmic Heat Sink

The 2.7K deep-space temperature gradient provides essentially free cooling — the largest thermodynamic driver for efficient energy extraction.

Cost Advantage

Projected $0.002/kWh operational cost vs. $0.05–0.12/kWh terrestrial average — a 25-60x cost reduction for energy-intensive AI workloads.
